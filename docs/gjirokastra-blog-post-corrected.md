### Why are stone houses in Gjirokastra so important for investors?

Stone houses in Gjirokastra offer far more than charming architecture. They demonstrate durability, heritage, strength, and long-term investment potential. Built in the last century with traditional methods, they provide a unique blend of authenticity and stability that modern developments cannot match. Owning one means participating in a vibrant cultural heritage and landscape.

**Key benefits for investors:**
- **Lower maintenance costs** — Timeless stone construction requires less upkeep than modern materials.
- **Original architecture** — Traditional floor plans and authentic design appeal to heritage buyers and tourists.
- **Strong appreciation** — Long-term property value tends to increase continuously as supply is limited.
- **Sustainable living** — Stone is naturally energy-efficient, reducing heating and cooling costs.
- **Functional layouts** — Designed for multi-generational living and adaptable to boutique hospitality.

### How does UNESCO status affect property investments here?

UNESCO status provides a badge of honor and directly impacts property investments. It ensures that buildings, streets, and neighborhoods are protected by strict conservation rules, giving investors confidence that the area will retain its character and value. It also drives global awareness, tourist attraction, and cultural projects—all of which increase rental income and resale potential.

**Benefits from UNESCO status:**
- **Legal protection** — Property is safeguarded from inappropriate changes or unsightly development.
- **Higher rental demand and property prices** — Market values align with a globally recognized destination.
- **International attention** — Attracts tourists and property investors from around the world.
- **Support for historic structures** — Access to restoration grants and preservation programs.

In short, **UNESCO status** adds credibility, long-term financial growth, and protection of Gjirokastra's properties.

### Additional important points to consider

**Rental income potential**
- Gjirokastra sees growing tourist numbers year-round. Short-term rentals (Airbnb-style) in restored stone houses can yield strong returns, especially during peak season (spring and autumn).

**Renovation and restoration**
- Many stone houses need restoration. Factor in renovation costs, but also consider grants or incentives available for UNESCO-protected buildings.

**Legal and ownership**
- Albania allows foreign nationals to buy property. Consult a local lawyer to handle titles and any historic-building restrictions.

**Market outlook**
- Compared to other European UNESCO sites, Gjirokastra still offers relatively affordable entry points with strong upside as infrastructure and tourism develop.
